They're hiding in the sentence

In Plain Sight takes a familiar title — a song, a film, a sports team — and hides it inside a completely natural-sounding sentence. Your job is to find it. No scrambled letters, no anagrams. Just a title embedded in text, waiting to be seen.

The mechanic is deceptively simple. The execution is fiendishly clever. When you finally see it, you'll wonder how you missed it the first time.

Find the hidden title:

"She said the hotel california weather was perfect — warm, dry, and nothing like home."

All puzzles are trickier than this one.
